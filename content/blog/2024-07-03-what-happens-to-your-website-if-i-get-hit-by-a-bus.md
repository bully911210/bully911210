Every business owner who has dealt with a technology vendor has, at some point, wondered what would happen if that vendor disappeared. It is not a morbid question. It is a practical one. What happens to your website if I get hit by a bus?

The answer to this question is one of the most important things I can tell you about how the SA Business Online Starter is structured, because it is also the answer to a quieter version of the same question: what happens to your website if I raise my prices, or change my terms, or decide to do something else with my life?

---

## The dependency trap most web solutions create

Wix creates a dependency. Your site lives in Wix's proprietary system. You cannot export it in a format that another platform or developer can use. If Wix ceases operations (unlikely but theoretically possible), your site disappears with it. If Wix restructures its pricing, you pay or you start over.

Many web developers create a similar dependency, less obviously. They build sites on platforms they control, or they hold credentials that give them more access than the client has. They make themselves essential to ongoing maintenance by building in complexity that only they can manage. This is not always malicious. It is often just the result of developers who have not thought carefully about who the site belongs to.

WordPress, built correctly, eliminates this dependency entirely.

---

## Why WordPress is structurally different

WordPress is open-source software. This means two things that matter for your situation.

First, it is not owned by anyone. It is maintained by a global community of developers and it has been since 2003. It is not going away. It is the most widely deployed website software in the world, running approximately 43 percent of all websites on the internet. No single person's disappearance can affect it.

Second, a WordPress installation is a portable, self-contained entity. The site's files, database, images, and configuration live in standard formats that any developer in the world can read, modify, host, and maintain. If I disappear tomorrow, the site does not disappear. It continues exactly as it is, because it is running on infrastructure registered in your name.

---

## What the backup means concretely

Every SA Business Online Starter site is backed up automatically every 24 hours using UpdraftPlus, with the backup stored to your personal Google Drive. Not my Google Drive. Yours.

This means that at any moment, you have a complete copy of your website: the database (which contains all your pages, posts, and content), the theme files, the plugin configurations, and all uploaded images. The backup is readable by any WordPress developer on the planet.

If I am unreachable for any reason, you can take that backup to any WordPress hosting provider and any WordPress developer and have a fully functional site restored in approximately 15 to 30 minutes. The plugins I use (Rank Math, WP Rocket, Google Site Kit) are widely documented and have large user communities. Any competent WordPress developer can work with them.

## The credentials you own

When I set up your site, the following accounts are registered in your name, with your email address, controlled by you:

Your domain registrar account. The .co.za or .com domain you choose is registered through your own account. You own the domain. I do not hold it on your behalf.

Your hosting account. The server that runs your site is a hosting account in your name. If you want to move hosting providers or manage the account yourself, you have the login.

Your Google accounts. Your Google Search Console, Google Analytics, and Google Site Kit are all connected to your own Google account. The data belongs to you and is accessible to you directly, without me in the loop.

Your WordPress admin login. You receive this before I build a single page. You are the administrator of your own site from day one.

The only thing I control in your setup is the maintenance access I use to apply updates and monitor security. That access is mine to facilitate the R299 monthly service. If you ever want to manage maintenance yourself or engage another provider, you can revoke my access with two clicks.

## Why this matters more than the "hit by a bus" scenario

The bus scenario is the edge case. The more likely scenario is simpler: you want to add a new page and you cannot reach me on the day you need it done. Or you want to change your services and I am busy with other clients. Or you simply want to understand what you own.

With the SA Business Online Starter, your website is yours. You can log in, make changes, add content, update your services, and manage it entirely on your own if you choose. WordPress has a straightforward content editor (the Gutenberg block editor) that most business owners can learn to use for basic updates within an hour.

You are not dependent on me to make your site function. I am available to help and to manage the technical maintenance, but you are not held hostage to my availability for changes to your own content.

## The question that tests every vendor

When you are evaluating any technology service or vendor, I think this question is worth asking explicitly: "What happens to what I have built if you are no longer available?"

If the honest answer to that question is "I am not sure" or "you would need to find someone who knows our system," that is information worth having before you sign anything.

The honest answer to that question for the SA Business Online Starter is: nothing changes. Your site continues to run on infrastructure you own, backed up to storage you control, using software that is maintained by the global WordPress community and accessible to any developer in the world.

That is not a marketing claim. It is a structural fact about how the service is built.
