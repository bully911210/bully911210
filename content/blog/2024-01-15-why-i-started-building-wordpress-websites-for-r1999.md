## Here is why I decided to change the number

I have been building WordPress websites professionally for eight years. I know what the components cost. I know what takes time and what does not. I know that if you strip out the account management overhead, the sales commission, the fancy offices, and the proposal process, a genuinely good, technically correct, conversion-ready WordPress website for a South African small business costs a specific amount of money to build.

That number is not R15,000.

The agencies charge R15,000 because they have to cover their costs. I charge R1,999 because I have engineered the process to the point where I can deliver the right outcome at the right price, without cutting corners on the things that actually matter.

Let me be clear about what "the things that actually matter" means, because this is where most affordable website services get it wrong.

A website that matters scores above 85 on Google PageSpeed Mobile. The average Wix site in South Africa scores between 35 and 55. I know this because I have checked hundreds of them. A score below 65 suppresses your Google ranking directly. It is not a suggestion from Google. It is an algorithm penalty applied automatically to every slow site.

A website that matters has LocalBusiness schema markup. This is a piece of invisible code that tells Google's systems not just that a page exists, but that a named, located, operating business exists at a specific address in a specific suburb. Without it, you are a generic page in Google's eyes. With it, you are a business that Google can confidently recommend when someone searches for your service in your area.

A website that matters has a contact form that actually delivers your leads. This sounds obvious. It is not. Forms that route mail through shared hosting without an SMTP relay have a 40 to 60 percent spam delivery rate. I have seen business owners who thought they were getting no enquiries when in fact they were getting enquiries that were disappearing into spam folders. I route all forms to WhatsApp and to a properly configured SMTP relay, so nothing gets lost.

None of these things require a R15,000 budget. They require knowing what you are doing.
