The most common question I receive, after people see the price, is some version of: "Why is it so affordable? What is the catch?"

It is a fair question. South Africa has enough experience with things that sound too good to be true that every reasonable person has developed a filter for them. I have the same filter. I would ask the same question.

Let me give you the honest answer.

---

## What the price is not

The price is not low because I cut corners on the technical side. The sites I build score between 88 and 95 on Google PageSpeed Mobile. They have LocalBusiness schema configured for the client's specific trade, location, and service area. They have Rank Math for SEO, WP Rocket for speed, and Google Site Kit for measurement, all installed and configured correctly. They have contact forms that route to WhatsApp in real time.

These are not things you get from the R800 nephew option. If the price were low because quality was low, I would not have 67 builds and zero refunds. People would have noticed.

The price is also not low because the monthly fee is hiding the real cost. The R299 per month covers hosting on reliable infrastructure (Hetzner South Africa), daily backups to the client's Google Drive, maintenance updates, and security monitoring. It is not a platform fee that increases annually. It is not a subscription to a proprietary system. It is the cost of keeping a WordPress installation live, maintained, and secure.

---

## What the price actually reflects

I got the pricing wrong at first. I started higher. Not agency-level high, but higher than R1,999. I was pricing based on what I thought the market expected to pay for quality, rather than on what the process actually costs.

The process costs less than most developers charge because of two things: standardisation and focus.

I build one type of site. Not e-commerce sites with custom product catalogues and payment gateways. Not property listing portals. Not SaaS platforms. I build local service business websites for South African small business owners. Plumbers, therapists, coaches, accountants, beauty professionals, contractors. The requirements are specific and consistent. Services listed. Location established. Contact form to WhatsApp. PageSpeed above 85. Schema configured. Done.

Because I have built 67 of these, the process is efficient in a way that a developer who builds three different types of sites per year cannot match. The decisions are made. The configurations are documented. The intake process is clear. The tools are chosen. The result is consistent.

Agencies charge what they charge because they have account managers, sales teams, project managers, physical offices, and a cost structure that requires a higher price. I have none of those things. I have a process that works and a product that delivers.

---

## I will tell you exactly what it costs me to build one

This is the level of transparency I believe an honest business should be willing to offer.

Hosting for one year: approximately R600 to R800 on a reliable South African server.

The WordPress plugins (Rank Math Pro, WP Rocket): I pay annual licence fees for these and spread the cost across builds. Per build, approximately R200.

Domain registration: approximately R150 for a .co.za.

Time: a standard build, from intake to handover, takes me between 8 and 12 hours. If you valued my time at a full agency rate of R800 per hour, a 10-hour build would cost R8,000. But I am not an agency. I am a single operator with a streamlined process and no overhead. My effective hourly rate on this service is deliberately kept accessible because the goal is volume and impact, not margin extraction on a small number of high-ticket projects.

The R1,999 once-off covers the hosting setup, the domain, the build time, and a 30-day guarantee period. The R299 per month covers ongoing hosting, maintenance, and security. Both numbers reflect what it actually costs to deliver this, with a margin that allows the service to continue operating, not a margin inflated by overhead that you are paying for indirectly.

---

## The part this is really about

I want to say something that is not quite a sales point.

South Africa has approximately 2.9 million small and medium enterprises. The majority of them do not have professional websites. Many of those that do have websites that are technically broken in ways the owners do not know about. The result is that the digital economy in South Africa, at the small business level, functions far below its potential.

I started this because I believed that a properly built website, accessible to any small business owner willing to invest R1,999, would change outcomes for those businesses in measurable ways. The case studies are evidence that this belief was correct.

This is not for businesses looking for the cheapest possible option. If R1,999 is a serious financial stretch, there are better places to start. This is for the business owner who has been putting off a professional website because of past bad experiences or because they believed quality required a R15,000 budget.

The price is what it is because of how I build, not despite what I build.
