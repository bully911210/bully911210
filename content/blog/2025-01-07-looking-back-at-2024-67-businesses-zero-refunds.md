This is the kind of post I would not normally write. Year-in-review content tends toward the self-congratulatory, and I do not think self-congratulatory is useful to anyone reading this.

But I want to be honest about what 2024 looked like for the SA Business Online Starter, partly because I think the numbers tell a genuine story about what is possible for South African small businesses online, and partly because I think documenting it publicly creates accountability.

So here it is: 67 websites built in 2024. Zero refunds claimed.

---

## The 67 number

I started 2024 having built 18 sites in the previous year. The word-of-mouth referral rate was higher than I had anticipated, and by March it was clear that the model was working at a rate I had not planned for.

The 67 builds in 2024 spanned:

14 trade and technical service businesses (plumbers, electricians, HVAC installers)
11 beauty and wellness businesses (salons, aestheticians, yoga instructors, massage therapists)
9 professional services (accountants, financial advisers, bookkeepers)
8 health and therapy practices (psychologists, physiotherapists, occupational therapists, life coaches)
7 real estate professionals
5 legal and consulting professionals
13 other local service businesses across various categories

The geographic spread was also wider than the previous year: more Cape Town and Durban builds, fewer exclusively Pretoria and Johannesburg builds.

## The zero-refunds number

I want to say something careful about this figure, because I think it is easy to misread.

Zero refunds does not mean every client was entirely satisfied with every aspect of every project. It does not mean there were no difficult moments or no revisions required or no builds that took longer than they should have. Building 67 websites involves a considerable number of human interactions, and human interactions are not uniformly smooth.

What zero refunds means is that in every case, the final product delivered what the service described. Every site launched with a mobile PageSpeed score above 85. Every site had LocalBusiness schema configured. Every contact form was routed to WhatsApp and confirmed delivering. Every client had their login before the intake call. Every handover session happened. Every backup was confirmed delivering to the client's Google Drive.

The guarantee was available to every client for 30 days. None of them used it. That is the correct reading of the zero.

## What the data shows about outcomes

I do not systematically collect outcome data from all clients because that would require a level of follow-up and reporting infrastructure that I have not yet built. What I have is anecdotal data from clients who have shared their outcomes with me directly, and data from the clients whose Google Site Kit dashboards I have visibility into during the maintenance period.

The patterns I have observed consistently:

Sites that launched with PageSpeed scores above 90 showed significant Search Console impression growth within 45 days. The average time to first organic enquiry through the website was 23 days for sites above 90, compared to 44 days for sites in the 82 to 89 range.

Contact form submissions through WhatsApp produced a significantly higher client response rate than the same submissions through email. Clients who received WhatsApp notifications responded to enquiries within an average of 12 minutes. The same enquiries sent by email, in the few builds where I tested the comparison, received responses averaging 4 hours. The conversion rate from initial enquiry to consultation or booking was meaningfully higher for the 12-minute response group.

The case studies in this category (Jaco, Priya, Nomsa, André, Sipho) are representative, not exceptional. The outcomes described in those posts are within the normal range of what correctly built sites produce for the relevant client categories in South Africa.

## What I am changing in 2025

One thing.

The onboarding process is becoming more structured. In 2024, the handover session was thorough but ad hoc. In 2025, every new client will receive a structured dashboard orientation as part of the handover: a documented walkthrough of their Google Site Kit data, what each metric means for their specific business category, and a simple action list for the first 30 days after launch.

The reason for this change is the observation I described in the "what I learned after 40 websites" post. The clients who get the most from their sites are the ones who engage with their data. Engagement increases when people understand what they are looking at. A more structured handover produces better-informed clients, and better-informed clients see better outcomes.

I am not changing the price. R1,999 once-off, R299 per month. This is the number that makes the service accessible to the business owners who need it, and it is the number that allows me to build at volume with quality maintained. Raising it would reduce the reach of the service without producing better outcomes.

## What comes next in South African small business websites

The window I described in the SEO post is still open, but it is narrowing. The number of South African small businesses with correctly built websites is increasing year by year. The competition for local search rankings in categories like plumbing, beauty, and professional services in major cities will be meaningfully higher in 2026 than it was in 2024.

The businesses that build correctly this year will have domain authority and ranking history advantages over the businesses that build in 2026 or later. This is not speculation. It is how Google's ranking algorithm works: older domains with consistent signals rank above newer domains with identical signals.

If you have been considering this and have been putting it off, I would rather tell you that honestly than soften it into a fake CTA.
