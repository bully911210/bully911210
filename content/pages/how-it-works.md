## Who This Is For

This page is for the business owner who has either been burned by a developer before and wants to know exactly what happens before committing, or who has never had a website and does not know what to expect from the process.

Either way, the answer is the same: the process is specific, short, and structured so that you own everything before I build anything.

Here is the exact sequence, step by step.

---

## Step 1: You book and pay

You go to the booking page and pay R1,999. The payment is processed securely. You receive an immediate booking confirmation email.

Within two hours of payment, a second email arrives with your WordPress administrator login.

This is not a login to a staging environment that I control. It is administrator access to a WordPress installation on a hosting account registered in your name. You own it from this point. You can log in, look around, and confirm that what I described is real before we have spoken once.

Most developers do not work this way. Most developers treat delivery as the moment when they hand over the product. I treat booking as the moment when I begin building something you already own.

---

## Step 2: We have a 30-minute intake call

I reach out within 24 hours of your booking to schedule the intake call. The call takes 30 minutes. No longer. No shorter.

I ask you the following:

What are your main services? Be specific. "Geyser replacement" is more useful than "plumbing." "Anxiety therapy" is more useful than "therapy." The specificity of the terms you use here determines the specificity of the search terms your site can rank for.

Where are you based and what suburbs do you serve? Your primary location and your service area both go into your schema markup. I need postcode-level accuracy here, not just city names.

Who is your ideal client? This is not a marketing exercise. It is a content brief. The language I use on your services page should match the language your ideal client uses when searching for you.

Do you have photos? A logo? Any existing content you want to retain? None of these are required, but each one saves time in the build.

Is there anything specific about your business that most websites in your category get wrong? This is always the most useful question and the one most clients answer with something they have never said aloud before.

That is the entire intake call. 30 minutes. Everything I need.

---

## Step 3: I build your site

The build takes three days from the intake call.

Day one: I set up the site structure, configure the theme, install and configure all plugins (Rank Math, WP Rocket, Google Site Kit, UpdraftPlus, WPForms), and connect the domain. The technical foundation is complete by the end of day one.

Day two: I build the content pages. Homepage, services page, about page, contact page. I write or adapt the content based on our intake call. If you have sent me photos or a logo, they go in. LocalBusiness schema is configured for your specific trade, location, service area, and operating hours.

Day three: I run the PageSpeed audit, confirm the WhatsApp form routing is delivering correctly, test all links, and do a final review. I set up the Google Drive backup and confirm it is delivering to your account. The backup runs once before I send you the review link so you can confirm it is working.

---

## Step 4: You review and approve

I send you a staging link to review the site before it goes live. This is not a soft "let me know if you have any changes." This is a formal review step. If something is wrong, I change it. If the content on any page does not reflect what you told me in the intake call, I rewrite it. If you want a section added or a page restructured, we do it before launch.

Most clients come back with minor changes: a service name phrased differently, an additional suburb added, a photo swapped. Occasionally a more substantial change is needed. Either way, nothing goes live until you have confirmed it is right.

---

## Step 5: Launch and handover

We book a 30 to 45-minute handover session. This happens on the launch day or within 24 hours of it.

In the handover, we go through:

Your WordPress dashboard: where things are, how to add or update content, how to check your Google Site Kit data.

Your Google Site Kit data: what the metrics mean, what to expect in weeks one through eight, and what one or two actions in the first 30 days are most likely to accelerate your results.

Your Google Business Profile: if you do not have one, we create it together during the handover. If you have one, we connect it correctly to the new site.

Your first 30-day action list: specific, simple, relevant to your business category and location. Not a list of generic SEO tips. A list of two or three things that, based on experience from similar builds, are most likely to move your numbers.

After the handover, the site is yours. I stay available for monthly maintenance on the R299 plan. You have a WhatsApp number and an email address and I respond to both.

If the site has a technical problem in the first 30 days, I fix it at no additional charge. This is part of the service, not an extra.
