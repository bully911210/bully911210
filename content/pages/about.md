## Who This Page Is For

If you are a South African small business owner who has been putting off a professional website because the quotes you have seen were unaffordable, because a previous developer left you with something that did not work, or because you have tried Wix and gotten nothing from it, this page is for you.

If you are looking for the cheapest possible website and quality does not concern you, this page is not for you. I would rather tell you that upfront than waste both our time.

## Who I Am

My name is Ruan Botha. I have been building WordPress websites for eight years. Before I started the SA Business Online Starter, I built websites in the traditional agency model: proposals, briefs, design presentations, account managers, invoices that justified the R12,000 to R20,000 price tag.

I got good at that work. I also got increasingly frustrated by the clients I was not reaching.

Most South African small business owners cannot spend R15,000 on a website. Not because they do not care about having one, but because R15,000 is a significant proportion of a quarter's revenue for a plumber in Centurion or an accountant in Tygervalley. So they went without. Or they got a nephew's Wix template. Or they paid R800 for something half-finished that sat broken for a year.

I knew what a good website actually cost to build when you stripped out the agency overhead. I knew that the things that make a website actually work (technical speed, correct schema markup, reliable lead delivery, proper measurement) were not the things that made websites expensive. The expensive part was the overhead, not the work.

So in 2022, I restructured what I built and how I built it, set the price at R1,999, and started offering what I was calling the SA Business Online Starter.

---

## What I Got Wrong

I underestimated how much the handover mattered. Early builds, I treated the handover as a practical information transfer. Here are your logins. Here is how to add a post. Goodbye.

What I learned over the first 20 builds was that clients who understood what they were looking at in their Google Site Kit dashboard were the clients who got the best outcomes. The site was not a finished product. It was the beginning of a relationship between a business owner and their own digital presence. The handover was the beginning of that relationship, not the end of the project.

I changed the handover process around build number 25. Every client now leaves the handover session knowing what their dashboard shows, what the numbers mean for their specific business, and what one or two actions they can take in the first 30 days that are likely to make the biggest difference. That change, more than any technical improvement, produced better client outcomes.

## What I Believe About This Work

I believe that most South African small business owners are doing genuinely good work that not enough people know about. The constraint is not quality. It is discoverability.

I believe that a correctly built website, technically sound and properly connected to Google's tools, is the most cost-effective investment most local service businesses can make. Not social media ads. Not a marketing retainer. A one-time investment in a website that works correctly.

I believe this not as a marketing position but as a conclusion from data. In 67 builds, across plumbers and therapists and accountants and salon owners and real estate agents, the pattern is consistent: when the technical foundation is correct, the organic search traffic comes, and the enquiries follow.

I also believe in building things that belong to the client from day one. Every client receives their WordPress login before I have built a single page. The site is theirs from the moment I set up the hosting. I am building something on their property, not mine.

## What I Am Building Toward

The SA Business Online Starter was originally built for a specific gap: South African small business owners who needed a professional, technically correct website but could not afford the agency model.

That gap is larger than I thought. South Africa has millions of small and medium businesses. Most of them do not have professional websites that actually work. The R1,999 price point, combined with the quality of the technical foundation, is producing outcomes that I think justify expanding this as far as possible.

The goal is not to build a web agency. It is to make the tool that changes how a business operates in its local market accessible to as many South African small business owners as possible.

67 builds and zero refunds is the current state of that goal. It is a number I want to continue increasing.
